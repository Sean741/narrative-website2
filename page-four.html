Wine Routes

The wine routes of Cape Town are well known for their stunning scenery, ancient vineyards, and superb wines.

Top 6 Vineyards

Groot Constantia

Groot Constantia in Constantia, South Africa's oldest wine estate, boasts stunning settings, award-winning wines, and a historic ambience.

Delaire Graff Estate

Delaire Graff Estate, a winery in Stellenbosch, mixes magnificent wines with eye-catching artwork, architectural details, and expansive views.

Steenberg Vineyards

Waterford Estate

Waterford Estate, located in Stellenbosch's Blaauwklippen Valley, is well-known for its Bordeaux-style blends.

Babylonstoren

Babylonstoren in the Drakenstein Valley is more than just a winery; it's a full experience with a stunning garden.

Klein Constantia

Klein Constantia in Constantia offers a historic setting and a selection of highly regarded wines, and is well-known for its renowned Vin de Constance dessert wine.

So Where Are They?

The vineyards are situated close to Cape Town, South Africa, in well-known wine-producing areas. Babylonstoren is in the Drakenstein Valley close to Paarl, Groot Constantia and Steenberg Vineyards are in Constantia, and Delaire Graff Estate is in Stellenbosch.

Cape Town Wines

Chenin Blanc

Cape Town's Chenin Blanc: Versatile, crisp white wine with refreshing acidity, showcasing diverse flavors beautifully.

Pinotage

Known for its unusual profile, Pinotage is a bold red wine from South Africa with deep colours, intense flavours, and unique character.

Shiraz

South African expressiveness is a full-bodied red wine with complex layers, spice, and robust fruit.

Sauvignon Blanc

Crisp Cape Town white, vibrant acidity, tropical fruit aromas, and refreshing herbaceous notes abound.
